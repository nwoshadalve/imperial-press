## 1. What is Imperial Press?

Imperial Press is an open-access academic publishing platform. It allows researchers from around the world to read published research papers and submit their own work to peer-reviewed journals. Once a paper is accepted and published, anyone can read it for free — there are no paywalls.

The platform hosts multiple journals, each covering a specific academic subject area. Every journal publishes issues on a regular schedule, and each issue contains a collection of research papers.

---

## 2. Who Uses the Platform?

Imperial Press serves four types of people:

**Readers**
Anyone who visits the website to browse and read research papers. No account is needed. Readers can search for papers, browse journals, download PDFs, and view article information — all without logging in.

**Authors**
Researchers who register on the platform to submit their papers to a journal. After submission, authors track the progress of their paper through review and publication via their personal dashboard.

**Reviewers**
Academic experts who are invited by the editorial team to evaluate submitted papers. They provide professional feedback that helps editors decide whether a paper should be published. Reviewers are recruited by the journal and their accounts are created by the admin after reviewing an application.

**Admins**
The internal team at Imperial Press who manage all content on the platform — journals, papers, blog posts, services, and user accounts. Admins also manage the submission and review process, confirm payments, and publish papers.

## 4. Journal Structure

### How Journals are Organised

Every journal on the platform belongs to a **Subject** (for example, "Linguistics and Translation"). Under each subject there can be multiple journals. Each journal publishes papers on a defined set of topics within that subject.

The structure of content is:

```
Subject Area
  └── Journal
        └── Volume  (one per year)
              └── Issue  (one per month)
                    └── Paper
```

**Volume and Issue Numbering**

A journal's Volume number counts up from the year it was founded. The Issue number corresponds to the month of publication (1 through 12). For example, a journal founded in 2024 publishing in July 2026 would produce **Volume 3, Issue 7**.

### Each Journal's Page

When a visitor opens a journal's page they find a full navigation menu covering everything about that journal:

**Journal Overview section (grouped together):**
- **About Journal** — history, editor-in-chief, open access statement
- **Aims & Scope** — what topics and types of research the journal accepts, the types of articles it publishes (research articles, review articles, book reviews, discussion notes, translated articles), and which languages it accepts
- **Peer Review Process** — how submissions are evaluated and what authors can expect
- **Author's Guidelines** — detailed instructions for preparing and formatting a manuscript
- **Article Processing Charges** — the cost to publish, what is included, and the Fast Track option
- **Publication Ethics** — the journal's standards on plagiarism, authorship, conflicts of interest, and retractions
- **Policies** — open access policy, self-archiving policy, data sharing, corrections
- **Frequently Asked Questions** — common questions answered

**Standalone sections:**
- **Why Publish With Us?** — reasons to choose this journal
- **Editorial Team** — photos and profiles of the editor-in-chief, associate editors, and editorial board members; includes a "Become a Reviewer" invitation at the bottom
- **Abstracting & Indexing** — all academic databases where the journal is listed (e.g. Google Scholar, EBSCO, ResearchGate)
- **Latest Issue** — the most recently published issue with all its papers
- **All Issues** — an archive of every past issue, grouped by year
- **Call for Papers** — open invitations for specific themes or special issues, with deadlines
- **Publishing Packages** — any bundled deals or institutional packages available
- **Google Scholar Citations** — a link to the journal's Google Scholar profile and its citation metrics
- **Announcements** — news specific to this journal
- **Contact Us** — how to reach the editorial team for this journal

A **Quick Links** panel on the side of the journal page gives fast access to the most important sections, and a prominent **Submit Your Paper** button is always visible.

## 5. Reading a Paper

When a visitor opens a paper, they see:

- The paper title
- The names and institutional affiliations of all authors
- The abstract
- Clickable keywords (clicking a keyword shows other papers tagged with the same word)
- Article information:
  - Which journal it was published in
  - Volume and issue number
  - DOI (a permanent link to the paper)
  - Page numbers
  - Publication date
  - Copyright statement
  - Licence (e.g. Creative Commons Attribution 4.0)
- An Open Access badge if the paper is freely available
- A button to download the PDF

Every time someone opens the paper page the view count increases by one. Every time someone downloads the PDF the download count increases by one.

## 7. Submitting a Paper

### Before Submitting

Authors submit papers at no cost. If a paper is accepted after peer review, an Article Processing Charge (APC) is invoiced at that point. Authors never pay unless their paper is accepted. This is the standard model used across the academic publishing industry.

To begin a submission the author must be logged in and have no unpaid outstanding invoices.

### Starting a Submission

An author can begin a submission by clicking "Submit Your Paper" on a journal's page, or by clicking "New Submission" on their dashboard. If they start from a journal page, that journal is pre-selected.

### Pre-Check

Before the submission begins, the author sees a pre-check screen where they:
- Confirm which journal they are submitting to
- Select the article type (Research Article, Review Article, Book Review, Discussion Note, or Translated Article)
- Tick a checklist confirming their submission meets the journal's requirements (originality, formatting, file type, etc.)
- Give privacy consent

Only after completing this can the submission begin.

### The Five-Step Submission Process

The submission is a guided five-step process. Progress is shown at the top of the screen. The author can save their work at any time and return to it later — the draft is saved automatically every 30 seconds.

**Step 1 — Details**
The author enters the paper title, keywords, and abstract.

**Step 2 — Upload Files**
The author uploads the manuscript file (Microsoft Word format only) and any supplementary files. The platform checks that the main file is in the correct format and rejects it immediately if not.

**Step 3 — Contributors**
The submitting author is already added. They can add co-authors by entering each person's name, email, institution, country, and role. Co-authors receive an email confirming their inclusion. The order of contributors can be rearranged by dragging and dropping.

**Step 4 — For the Editors**
The author can leave an optional message for the editorial team, suggest reviewers they would like to be considered, or name any reviewers they would prefer not be used (with a reason).

**Step 5 — Review and Submit**
A full summary of everything entered in the previous steps is shown. The author can go back and edit any section. When satisfied, they click Submit.

After submitting:
- A unique Submission ID is generated (e.g. `IJLLT-2026-00142`)
- A confirmation email is sent to the author and all co-authors
- The editorial team is notified
- The author sees a confirmation screen with links to review the submission, start a new one, or return to their dashboard

### Tracking a Submission

Authors track their submissions through the dashboard. Each submission shows one of the following statuses:

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | The submission is in progress and has not been submitted yet |
| **Submitted** | The submission is complete and waiting for an editor to review it |
| **Under Review** | The editor has assigned reviewers and peer review is in progress |
| **Revision Requested** | The editor has asked the author to revise the paper |
| **Accepted** | The paper has passed peer review and been accepted for publication |
| **Payment Pending** | The APC invoice has been issued and is awaiting payment |
| **Payment Confirmed** | Payment has been received and the paper is scheduled for publication |
| **Published** | The paper is live on the journal |
| **Rejected** | The paper was not accepted |

### Revising a Paper

When a revision is requested, the author receives an email with the editor's comments and the anonymised feedback from reviewers. The dashboard shows an "Upload Revision" option. The author uploads the revised manuscript and writes a response to the editor, then resubmits.

## 8. Peer Review Process

Imperial Press uses **double-blind peer review** as the default for all journals. This means:
- Reviewers do not know who the authors are
- Authors do not know who the reviewers are
- Only the editorial team sees both sides

The number of reviewers per paper defaults to two but can be adjusted per journal. The review deadline defaults to 21 days from the date a reviewer accepts the invitation.

### How it Works

**Step 1 — Scope Check**
When a submission arrives, the editor first checks whether it falls within the scope of the journal. If it clearly does not, the editor can reject it at this stage (a "desk rejection") and notify the author with a reason.

**Step 2 — Assigning Reviewers**
If the paper is within scope, the editor selects two or more reviewers from the reviewer database. The editor can filter reviewers by their subject expertise, availability, which journals they are assigned to, and how many active reviews they currently have.

**Step 3 — Review Invitations**
Selected reviewers receive an invitation email showing the abstract (without author names). They can accept or decline. If a reviewer declines, the editor selects a replacement. Once a reviewer accepts, they can download the anonymised manuscript and begin their evaluation.

**Step 4 — The Review**
The reviewer reads the manuscript and completes a review form with:
- An overall recommendation: Accept, Minor Revision, Major Revision, or Reject
- Confidential comments to the editor (not shared with the author)
- Substantive feedback for the author
- Optionally, an annotated version of the manuscript with tracked changes

**Step 5 — Editorial Decision**
Once all reviewers have submitted their reports (or the deadline has passed), the editor reads the reports and makes a final decision:
- **Accept** — the paper is accepted; the APC payment process begins
- **Minor or Major Revision** — the editor sends anonymised reviewer feedback to the author and asks for a revised version by a set deadline
- **Reject** — the editor sends a rejection email with comments

**Automatic reminders** are sent to reviewers 7 days before their deadline, 1 day before, and on the day the deadline passes. The editor is also alerted if a review becomes overdue.

## 9. Reviewer Accounts

### Becoming a Reviewer

Reviewers are not self-registered. Anyone interested in becoming a reviewer for a journal downloads a Reviewer Application Form from the journal's Editorial Team page, fills it out, and emails it to the journal's editorial address.

The Imperial Press team reviews the application and, if approved, creates a reviewer account manually. The reviewer receives a welcome email with their login credentials.

### Reviewer Profile

Reviewers can update their bio, research expertise areas, and availability status (Available or Temporarily Unavailable) from their profile.

### Reviewer Dashboard

After login, reviewers see their dedicated dashboard showing:

- **Pending Invitations** — review requests they have not yet responded to, showing the abstract, journal, and deadline
- **Active Reviews** — reviews they have accepted and are working on, with a countdown to the deadline
- **Completed Reviews** — a history of all past reviews
- **My Certificates** — Peer Review Certificates they have earned, available to download
- **My Profile** — expertise and availability settings

A reviewer who is also an author will see both dashboards side by side after login.

## 10. Article Processing Charges & Payment

### The Standard APC

Each journal has a defined Article Processing Charge (APC) — for example, $150 USD. This fee is charged **only after a paper has been accepted**, never at submission. It covers:

1. Editorial and peer review services
2. Online hosting and maintenance
3. DOI (permanent identifier) assignment
4. A formal acceptance letter
5. A publication certificate
6. Indexing in academic databases
7. Plagiarism screening and report
8. Open access provision (free reading for everyone)
9. Long-term article archiving
10. Marketing and promotion of the article

### The Fast Track Option

Authors who need an accelerated review can opt for Fast Track at $250 USD. This gives them priority handling from editors and reviewers. Acceptance is not guaranteed under Fast Track — the same quality standards apply.

### The Payment Process

Imperial Press currently uses a manual payment process:

1. When a paper is accepted, the author receives an email with the APC amount, a unique payment reference number, and payment instructions (bank transfer details or other methods defined by Imperial Press)
2. The author makes the payment through the stated method
3. The author logs into their dashboard and uploads proof of payment (a receipt or screenshot)
4. The Imperial Press admin team reviews the proof and confirms or queries the payment
5. Once confirmed, the author is notified and the paper moves to publication

If the payment proof is rejected (e.g. incorrect amount or missing reference number), the author is notified with the reason and asked to resubmit.

## 11. Certificates

Imperial Press issues three types of digital certificates, all available as downloadable PDF files.

### Acceptance Certificate

Issued to each author and co-author when their paper is accepted for publication. Useful for university promotion applications, travel grants, and visa applications where proof of acceptance is needed before a paper is published.

**Contains:** Author name, paper title, journal name, date of acceptance, ISSN numbers, a statement confirming the paper passed double-blind peer review, a unique Certificate ID, and the Editor-in-Chief's signature.

### Publication Certificate

Issued to each author and co-author when their paper is published. The formal confirmation of publication.

**Contains:** Author name, paper title, journal name, volume and issue, DOI, page numbers, publication date, ISSN numbers, impact factor, licence type, a unique Certificate ID, and the Editor-in-Chief's signature and journal seal.

### Peer Review Certificate

Issued to a reviewer when they complete and submit a review report. Useful for academics demonstrating peer review service to their institutions.

**Contains:** Reviewer name, journal name, date review was completed, a statement acknowledging their contribution, and a unique Certificate ID. The manuscript title is intentionally omitted to preserve the confidentiality of the review process.

### One Certificate Per Person

If a paper has three authors, three separate certificates are issued — one addressed to each author by name.

### Verifying a Certificate

Every certificate carries a unique Certificate ID and a link to `imperialpress.com/verify`. Anyone — an employer, university, or institution — can visit this page, enter the Certificate ID, and instantly confirm whether the certificate is genuine. The verification page shows the key facts without displaying any private personal information.

If a paper is ever retracted, its associated certificates can be revoked by the admin. A revoked certificate shows a "This certificate has been revoked" message on the verification page.

## 12. Content Management by Admin

Everything on the Imperial Press platform is managed by the admin team through a private admin panel. No developer is needed to manage day-to-day content.

### What Admin Can Manage

**Journals and Content**
- Create, edit, and delete academic subjects, journals, volumes, issues, and papers
- Set which journals are featured on the home page
- Write and update all journal page content (About, Aims & Scope, Peer Review Process, Guidelines, Ethics, Policies, FAQs, Why Publish With Us)
- Upload and manage editorial team member profiles per journal
- Manage indexing database entries and logos per journal
- Create and publish announcements per journal
- Create and manage Call for Papers entries per journal
- Create and manage Publishing Packages per journal
- Update the Google Scholar profile link per journal

**Blog and Site Pages**
- Write, edit, and publish blog posts
- Edit the About Us, Partners, Careers, Awards, and other static pages
- Create and manage service subpages using a block-based page builder (no coding required)

**Submission and Editorial Management**
- View all submissions and filter by status
- Assign editors to submissions
- Search and assign reviewers by expertise and availability
- Set review deadlines
- Read submitted reviewer reports
- Make editorial decisions (Accept, Request Revision, Reject)
- Send manual reminder emails to overdue reviewers
- Track revision submissions from authors

**Payments**
- Review author-submitted payment proofs
- Confirm or reject payments with a note
- Set APC amounts per journal (standard and Fast Track)
- Set payment instructions (bank details, payment methods)
- Set the payment deadline period

**Certificates**
- Upload certificate design templates per journal
- Upload the Editor-in-Chief's signature image per journal
- Upload the journal seal image per journal
- View all issued certificates
- Regenerate a certificate if needed
- Revoke a certificate with a reason

**User Accounts**
- View all registered authors and reviewers
- Deactivate accounts if needed
- Create reviewer accounts after approving applications
- Upload the reviewer application form per journal
- Upload manuscript templates per journal

**Email Templates**
- Edit the content of every automated email the system sends

## 13. Search

The platform has a full-text search that allows visitors to find papers and journals instantly as they type. Results appear immediately with no need to press a search button.

**What can be searched:**
- Papers — by title, abstract, keywords, author name, or journal name
- Journals — by name, subject area, or ISSN
- Blog posts — by title or excerpt

**Where search appears:**
- The home page — a prominent search bar for finding papers across all journals
- The journal listing page — to filter journals by name or subject

Results are grouped by type (Papers and Journals). Clicking a result takes the visitor directly to that paper or journal page.

Clicking any keyword tag on a paper page opens a search results page showing all other papers tagged with that same keyword.
